About Granny

Granny started baking pies in her small farmhouse kitchen in 1948. What began as a hobby to feed her large family quickly became a passion. Neighbors, friends, and eventually strangers from across the county would stop by just for a slice. Today, her recipes live on through this website so everyone can enjoy a taste of her legendary baking.

"A pie a day keeps the frowns away." — Granny

Baking Tips

New to pie baking? Here are a few of Granny's top tips:

  1. Always use cold butter when making pie crust for a flakier result.
  2. Let your pie cool completely before slicing to help it set properly.
  3. Fresh-squeezed lemon juice makes a noticeable difference in lemon desserts.
  4. When making meringue, make sure your bowl is completely grease-free.
